Quick and "Dirty" Research

Overview

We were looking to improve consumer usabiliy when adjusting speed and incline on digital cardio equipment consoles. With a limited budget, we wanted to keep costs low. We decided to go with paper prototypes taped to existing consoles and we needed to get a better impression of where our testers were pressing â€“ but, how? Ink? Water? Sticky Notes/Dots?

Finally, we had it. Fingerpaints! It's fun, easy to clean up and easy to apply.

We then created a script and ranking system for our researchers and ran our test subjects through three layout options.

Each paper prototype was affixed to the console and participants used finger paint to "adjust" the speed.

We then analyzed each participants sheet and remarks.
